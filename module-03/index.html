Computer Comparison

A hardware research assignment comparing three personal computers based on the needs of a real interviewee.

The Interview

I interviewed my younger brother, who is looking to purchase a new personal computer. He'll mainly be using it for school, he's a Social Work major, so he doesn't have major requirements, he just needs a reliable machine for editing and reading documents, and casual web browsing. He occasionally will use it to stream videos when he's at home. His main concerns were portability (he'll be taking it to school), a comfortable screen size, and a price range of $900–$1,500

Key requirements from the interview

  • Use: Microsoft Office, online courses, streaming
  • Portability: Needs to move around easily
  • Screen size: At least 13–14 inches for comfortable reading
  • Budget: $900–$1,500
  • Operating System: Prefers Windows (already familiar)

The Three Options

Option A

Dell Inspiron 14" Laptop

A dependable mid-range laptop with strong everyday performance, a full HD display, and solid battery life. Well-suited for office work and video calls.

Price: ~$949

View on Dell.com

Option B

Microsoft Surface Laptop 5 — 13.5"

A sleek, lightweight laptop with a premium touchscreen display and excellent keyboard. Runs Windows 11 natively and integrates well with Microsoft 365.

Price: ~$1,299

View on Microsoft.com

Option C

HP Pavilion x360 2-in-1 14"

A versatile 2-in-1 touchscreen laptop with a fast Intel Core 7 processor, 16 GB of RAM, and a backlit keyboard. Lightweight at 3.35 lb with up to 9 hours of battery life.

Price: ~$1,099

View on HP.com

Conclusion

Based on our interview, I would recommend Option C: the HP Pavilion x360 14" as the best fit for my brother's needs. As a 2-in-1 touchscreen laptop, it offers the most versatility for switching between typing and reading modes, which is useful for both coursework and video streaming. It matches the Dell and Surface on RAM (16 GB) and fits comfortably within budget at $1,099 (MSRP). The Intel Core 7 processor is the fastest of the three options, and the backlit keyboard, fingerprint reader, and 9-hour battery life make it practical for long homework study days. For someone who values flexibility and performance at a reasonable price, the HP Pavilion x360 is the strongest overall choice.
