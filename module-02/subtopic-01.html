Rivest-Shamir-Adleman (RSA) Algorithim

The RSA algorithm is named after Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Leonard Adleman, who originally published the algorithm in 1977. The RSA algorithm is an assymettric or public-key cryptogtaphy algorithm which means that it works using two different keys, a public key and a private key. The public key is used for encryption and is known by everyone, while the provate key is used for decryption, and is kept secret by the receiver.

The RSA algorithm is based on factorization of large numbers and modular arithemtic for encrypting and decrupting data. It consists of 3 main stages.
